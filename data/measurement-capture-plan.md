# Measurement Capture Plan

This file defines the minimum physical evidence needed before the steel tongue drum should be described as tuned or public-review ready.

## 1. Material confirmation

- Record supplier, part description, and best-available steel grade.
- Measure thickness at multiple points on the real blank.
- Note whether the build uses flat sheet, a preformed shell, a welded assembly, or salvage.

## 2. Cutting-process coupon

- Cut a coupon with the same process intended for the instrument.
- Record actual kerf width at several points.
- Photograph taper, burrs, discoloration, and any warp.
- Strike the coupon before and after deburring if the coupon geometry supports it.

## 3. Rough-cut first strike

- Capture first stable Hz for bass plus every numbered tongue.
- Keep the support condition, mallet, and microphone placement fixed.
- Log any tongue that is already sharp or unexpectedly quiet before tuning starts.

## 4. Tuning passes

- Record each material-removal pass and whether it changed pitch as expected.
- Log cents error before and after the pass.
- Note any tongue that becomes weak, rattly, or structurally suspect.

## 5. Mount and finish checks

- Compare temporary cradle readings to final-foot readings.
- Measure again after finish cure.
- Re-check after 24-72 hours of rest for drift.

## 6. Evidence bundle

Minimum evidence for a public-safe tuning claim:

- populated `validation.csv`
- updated `validation-report.md`
- process notes or photos for material, coupon, and first-strike stages
- explicit note of any remaining unknowns or revisions to the workbook assumptions
