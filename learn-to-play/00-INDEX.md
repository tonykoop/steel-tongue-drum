# Steel Tongue Drum — Starter Songbook

Your steel tongue drum is tuned to **D natural minor** (D Aeolian):

```
D3 (bass) — A3 — Bb3 — C4 — D4 — E4 — F4 — G4 — A4 — C5 — D5
```

The steel tongue drum plays by striking tongues cut into the top surface.
Striking speed, mallet type, and position all affect tone quality.

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: stepwise motion, slow tempo
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: larger leaps, some rhythmic complexity
- ★★★ — Advanced: full range, ornamentation

## Mallet and striking notes
- Soft rubber or silicone mallets produce the warmest sustained tone.
- Strike each tongue near its center (not at the tip or base of the slit).
- Harder sticks produce brighter, shorter tones; softer produce fuller bloom.
- The bass tongue (D3) is the foundation — use it as a drone for improvisation.
