# Notes: Sakura Sakura

**Provenance:** Traditional Japanese melody associated with cherry blossoms.
Origin: Edo period (17th–18th century). Published in "Kinkyoku Shūi" (1888), PD.
The famous koto version and all traditional arrangements are public domain.

**Range check:** A3–E4. Uses only 4 of the 11 drum tongues — perfect for beginners.

**Practice tips:**
1. The Bb (b in ABC K:Dmin) gives this tune its distinctly Japanese character — don't hurry past it.
2. Each Bb is approached from A (a half-step below) — listen for the tension and release.
3. At ♩= 72, count slowly: 1 – 2 – 3 – 4. Don't rush the whole notes (d2).
4. The bass D tongue can echo softly between phrases for ambience.
