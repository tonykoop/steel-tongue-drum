Steel Tongue Drum

Root-mode packet for a tuned steel tongue drum, with first-pass formulas and an empirical validation workflow.

Readiness: private-prototype ready, not public-review ready until measured tuning data exists.

What this is

This packet documents a 12 inch steel tongue drum concept from the existing workbook. It treats the cantilever model as a layout estimate and makes the measured tuning loop the center of the build.

The design

The baseline assumes a 12 inch diameter, 6 inch high steel shell, 0.079 inch steel, 0.040 inch kerf, 1 inch tongues, and A4 = 440 Hz. Final pitch depends on actual steel, cutting process, support, mallet, finish, and iterative tuning.

Tuning and validation

The required validation rows are already in the packet. The build should not be described as tuned until those rows contain measured Hz, cents error, support condition, mallet, and environment.

Known risks

Laser HAZ, waterjet taper, CNC fixturing, burr mass, stress relief, final mount damping, mallet hardness, and finish mass can all move pitch. The packet records each as a measurable risk rather than pretending the spreadsheet can solve it alone.
